### [1] [Controlled generation of 3D vortices in driven atomic Josephson junctions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.05645)
*Vijay Pal Singh,Ludwig Mathey,Herwig Ott,Luigi Amico*

Main category: cond-mat.quant-gas

TL;DR: 提出交流驱动原子约瑟夫森结作为量子流体中三维孤立波的清洁可调源，可产生从涡旋环到稀疏脉冲的完整Jones-Roberts孤子家族，通过Shapiro阶跃实现确定性发射。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为量子流体中的三维孤子激发提供可控、可重复的生成平台，以研究非线性涡旋动力学、耗散和量子湍流。

Method: 使用交流驱动的原子约瑟夫森结，通过调节结势垒高度，在不同Shapiro阶跃上确定性发射单个或多个孤子激发。

Result: 成功生成了从低速涡旋环到接近声速的无涡度稀疏脉冲的完整孤子谱系，并实现了单激发和多激发配置的受控生成。

Conclusion: 该交流驱动协议为生成、分类和控制三维孤子激发建立了紧凑可重复平台，为精密研究超流体中的非线性动力学开辟了新途径。

Abstract: We propose an ac-driven atomic Josephson junction as a clean and tunable
source of three dimensional (3D) solitary waves in quantum fluids. Depending on
the height of the junction barrier, the emitted excitations appear as vortex
rings at low velocity or vorticity-free rarefaction pulses near the sound
velocity, thus spanning the complete Jones-Roberts family of solitons. The
Shapiro-step phenomenon renders the emission deterministic: on the first,
second, third Shapiro steps, the junction ejects one, two, and three solitary
excitations per drive cycle. This enables controlled generation of single- and
multi-excitation configurations, allowing detailed studies of the full
crossover between vortex rings and rarefaction pulses and their interaction
dynamics. In particular, deterministic multi-ring emission provides insights
into leapfrogging dynamics of two and three coaxial rings and their decay via
boundary-assisted, sound-mediated processes. This ac-driven protocol
establishes a compact and reproducible platform for generating, classifying,
and controlling 3D solitonic excitations, paving the way for precision studies
of nonlinear vortex dynamics, dissipation, and quantum turbulence in trapped
superfluids.

### [2] [A passive atomtronics filter for Fermi gases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.06612)
*Jun Hao Hue,Martin-Isbjörn Trappe,Piotr T. Grochowski,Jonathan Lau,Leong-Chuan Kwek*

Main category: cond-mat.quant-gas

TL;DR: 设计了一种原子电子滤波器，通过哑铃形陷阱势场在二维几何中分离具有排斥接触相互作用的双组分费米气体，在强排斥状态下每个环形陷阱存储一种费米子种类。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为初始混合的弱排斥自旋组分设计组分滤波器，建立密度-势函数理论作为费米子原子电子学的综合模拟框架。

Method: 使用密度-势函数理论(DPFT)模拟费米气体在现实设置中的行为，设计并表征连接两个环形势场的桥形势场构成的哑铃形陷阱势。

Result: 在强排斥状态下，每个环形陷阱成功存储一种费米子种类，且该哑铃设计对各种实验设置变化具有鲁棒性。

Conclusion: 这项研究标志着建立DPFT作为费米子原子电子学综合模拟框架的第一步，证明了该滤波器设计的有效性和鲁棒性。

Abstract: We design an atomtronic filter device that spatially separates the components
of a two-component Fermi gas with repulsive contact interactions in a
two-dimensional geometry. With the aid of density--potential functional theory
(DPFT), which can accurately simulate Fermi gases in realistic settings, we
propose and characterize a barbell-shaped trapping potential, where a
bridge-shaped potential connects two ring-shaped potentials. In the strongly
repulsive regime, each of the ring traps eventually stores one of the fermion
species. Our simulations are a guide to designing component filters for
initially mixed, weakly repulsive spin components. We demonstrate that the
functioning of this barbell design is robust against variations in experimental
settings, for example, across particle numbers, for small deformations of the
trap geometry, or if interatomic interactions differ from the bare contact
repulsion. Our investigation marks the first step in establishing DPFT as a
comprehensive simulation framework for fermionic atomtronics.

</details>


### [3] [Broad Feshbach resonance with a large background scattering length in a fermionic atom-molecule mixture](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.06772)
*Zhen Su,Tong-Hui Shou,Huan Yang,Jin Cao,Bo-Yuan Wang,Ting Xie,Jun Rui,Bo Zhao,Jian-Wei Pan*

Main category: cond-mat.quant-gas

TL;DR: 在超冷费米子混合物中观察到一个具有大背景散射长度的宽磁Feshbach共振，该共振驱动原子-分子混合物进入流体动力学区域，并观察到相锁定的集体运动。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究具有质量不平衡的强相互作用费米子气体，探索原子-分子混合物的新型相互作用机制。

Method: 通过测量共振增强的损失率和弹性散射截面来表征Feshbach共振，利用交叉物种热化方法研究弹性散射。

Result: 观察到原子和分子的质心运动相位锁定并以共同频率振荡，这是由流体动力学拖曳效应引起的。

Conclusion: 这种具有大背景散射长度的宽原子-分子Feshbach共振为研究质量不平衡的强相互作用费米子气体开辟了新途径。

Abstract: We report the observation of a broad magnetic Feshbach resonance with a large
background scattering length in an ultracold fermionic mixture of
$^{23}$Na$^{40}$K molecules and $^{40}$K atoms, with both species prepared in
their lowest hyperfine states. The Feshbach resonance is characterized by
measuring resonantly enhanced loss rates and elastic scattering cross sections
via cross-species thermalization. The large background scattering length can
drive the atom-molecule mixture into the hydrodynamic regime when the magnetic
field is far from the resonance. We observe that the center-of-mass motions of
the atoms and molecules are phase-locked and oscillate with a common frequency
due to hydrodynamic drag effects. This broad atom-molecule Feshbach resonance
with its large background scattering length opens up a new avenue towards
studying strongly interacting fermionic gases with mass imbalance.

</details>


### [4] [Chiral phases and dynamics of dipoles in triangular optical ladders](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.07042)
*Arjo Dasgupta,Mateusz Łącki,Henning Korbmacher,Gustavo A. Domínguez-Castro,Jakub Zakrzewski,Luis Santos*

Main category: cond-mat.quant-gas

TL;DR: 该论文研究了三角形光学梯子中偶极子的几何阻挫与长程各向异性相互作用，发现了手性超流体和非手性双组分超流体之间的偶极诱导转变，以及在旋转分子系统中实现的手性和向列相等丰富基态相。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究几何阻挫与长程各向异性相互作用之间的相互作用，特别是观察手性的自发产生，为理解偶极系统在受限几何结构中的行为提供新见解。

Method: 使用三角形光学梯子中的偶极子作为平台，分析极化偶极玻色子的巡游行为和自旋-1/2偶极子的钉扎行为，通过控制外部电场强度和方向探索不同相。

Result: 发现偶极相互作用在阻挫系统中被放大，导致手性超流体向非手性双组分超流体的转变可在当前实验温度下观察到。旋转分子系统展现出包括手性相、向列相在内的丰富基态相图。

Conclusion: 三角形光学梯子中的偶极系统为研究几何阻挫与长程相互作用提供了灵活平台，能够实现手性相变和丰富的多体物理现象，具有重要的实验观测价值。

Abstract: Dipoles in triangular optical ladders constitute a flexible platform for the
study of the interplay between geometric frustration and long-range anisotropic
interactions, and in particular for the observation of the spontaneous onset of
chirality. Frustration magnifies the effect of the dipolar interactions in
itinerant polarized dipolar bosons. As a result, the dipole-induced transition
between a chiral superfluid and a non-chiral two-component superfluid may be
observed for current state-of-the-art temperatures even for the weak inter-site
interaction characterizing magnetic atoms in standard optical lattices. On the
other hand, pinned spin-$1/2$ dipoles, which we discuss in the context of polar
molecules in two rotational states, realize frustrated dipolar XXZ spin models.
By controlling the external electric field strength and orientation, these
systems can explore a rich ground-state landscape including chiral and nematic
phases, as well as intriguing chiral dynamics.

</details>
